# Simplified Chinese Hanzi Resources

## Knowledge

- [Article: "The most common Chinese words, characters and components for language learners and teachers" — Olle Linge, Hacking Chinese](https://www.hackingchinese.com/the-most-common-chinese-words-characters-and-components-for-language-learners-and-teachers/)
  Direct treatment of the exact problem driving this mission: why frequency lists alone are misleading and how to combine frequency with component/structural knowledge. Use for: sequencing decisions, justifying why we teach components before compounds.
- [Outlier Dictionary of Chinese Characters — Outlier Linguistics](https://www.outlier-linguistics.com/products/outlier-dictionary-of-chinese-characters)
  Built by a team of linguists on a "Functional Component Framework" (every component is classified as semantic, phonetic, form, or empty) instead of the traditional, often-misleading 214 Kangxi radical list. Available as a Pleco add-on. Use for: getting the *real* etymology/function of a component before teaching it, instead of folk explanations.
  Review: [Hacking Chinese review of the Outlier dictionary](https://www.hackingchinese.com/review-the-outlier-linguistics-dictionary-of-chinese-characters-with-discount-code/)
- [Jun Da Modern Chinese Character Frequency List — MTSU](https://lingua.mtsu.edu/chinese-computing/statistics/char/list.php?Which=MO)
  The standard academic frequency list, corpus-derived. Use for: deciding which characters/components earn a lesson slot soonest.
- [hanzidb.org — character list by frequency, with radical and stroke-count columns](http://hanzidb.org/character-list/by-frequency)
  Convenient queryable version of frequency + radical/component data side by side. Use for: quickly finding "what else uses this component" when planning a lesson cluster.
- [Wiktionary — Chinese character entries (e.g. en.wiktionary.org/wiki/木)](https://en.wiktionary.org/)
  Crowd-sourced but well-cited component/etymology breakdowns for individual characters, cross-checked against Outlier where possible. Use for: per-character glyph origin stories used as mnemonics.
- [Arch Chinese — stroke order rules](https://www.archchinese.com/chinese_stroke_order_rules.html)
  Clear writeup of the core stroke order rules under the 1997 mainland "Modern Chinese Commonly Used Character Stroke Order Standard." Use for: the stroke-order primers in early lessons.
- [StrokeOrder.com — stroke order dictionary/animations](https://www.strokeorder.com/)
  Look up any character for an animated stroke-by-stroke writing demo. Use for: handwriting practice once a character has been introduced (look the character up directly on the site rather than relying on a static lesson diagram).

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/ChineseLanguage](https://reddit.com/r/ChineseLanguage)
  Large, active subreddit for learners of all levels; weekly threads for resources and questions. Use for: sanity-checking mnemonics, asking about real-world usage nuance.
- [Chinese-Forums.com](https://www.chinese-forums.com/)
  One of the oldest, most comprehensive learner forums, with deep threads on characters and study methods. Use for: more in-depth/long-form discussion than Reddit, especially on study methodology.

# Mission: Simplified Chinese Hanzi

## Why
The user wants general practical literacy in simplified Chinese — being able to read signs, menus, and everyday text while travelling or living alongside Mandarin speakers. They already practice speaking/listening through other apps, but those apps teach multi-character words as opaque blobs to memorize, which doesn't stick. They want hanzi taught the way the writing system actually works: build up from radicals/components so that compound characters and words become *decomposable and inferable* rather than rote-memorized.

## Success looks like
- Recognize a growing set of high-frequency radicals/components and state their core meaning (and sound, where phonetic) on sight.
- Given an unfamiliar character, decompose it into known components and make a reasonable guess at its meaning and/or pronunciation category.
- Read common real-world text — signs, menus, transit, basic notices — in simplified characters.
- Write learned characters with correct stroke order, including the simplified-specific variant forms of components (e.g. 氵 vs 水).
- Have an expanding personal base of characters that started at ~50-150 (pre-existing, mostly from speaking-app flashcards) and grows in a way that's actually retained, not just briefly recognized.

## Constraints
- Lessons must be short and frequent: 5-10 minutes, one component or tightly related cluster at a time. Working-memory budget is small per session.
- Progression combines **component/radical structure** (so compounds are decomposable) with **frequency data** (so time goes to characters/components that pay off soonest) — not a strict Kangxi radical-chart order, and not a strict frequency-list order in isolation.
- Include stroke order basics for handwriting, not just recognition.
- This workspace is reading/writing-focused. Spoken/listening practice is already covered elsewhere — don't duplicate it.
- Simplified characters only. Traditional forms may be referenced briefly when they clarify a component's origin/etymology, but are not a teaching target.

## Out of scope
- Spoken pronunciation drilling, tones practice, listening comprehension — covered by the user's existing speaking-focused apps.
- Traditional character forms as a learning target.
- Formal HSK exam strategy/test-taking technique (the HSK frequency bands may still inform sequencing, per [[RESOURCES]], but passing the exam itself isn't the goal right now).
